Later in the year, the idea of some sort of public employment was again in the air.
Lady Cowper, for instance, told Princess Lieven on September 25th that Anglesey was
very much annoyed at not obtaining the Admiralty in place of Lord Auckland, who
had gone to govern India. If there was any truth in this, Lord Melbourne's letter of
the following day, offering Anglesey the Government of Gibraltar, may have been a
sop.
